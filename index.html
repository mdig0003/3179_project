What are Trends in Different Home Attributes?

In Melbourne, the pursuit of affordable housing is a multifaceted challenge, as reflected in key trends observed in the available data.

The scatterplot analysis reveals a noteworthy correlation between the number of bedrooms and home prices, with an increase in the number of bedrooms often resulting in higher property costs. What data shows that the most expensive home on average is highest with 6 bedrooms which is roughly $1,900,000.

Additionally, the scatterplot indicating that home prices tend to rise as properties draw closer to the city center underscores the impact of location on affordability.

How Much are Homes Purchased for in Metropolitan Melbourne?

In summary, the housing prices in Melbourne's different suburbs can be described as follows:

Eastern suburbs: Most homes are selling for up to approximately $800,000.

Western suburbs: Homes are generally priced at a maximum of around $650,000.

Northern suburbs: Homes typically sell for around $750,000.

Southern suburbs: Home prices range from approximately $600,000 to $800,000.

South Eastern suburbs: Most properties are selling for prices ranging from about $600,000 to $650,000.

What We can Conclude?

From the data provdied, we can show that the most affordable homes are found within northern and western suburbs of Melbourne.

The most expensive suburbs of Melbourne are found in the southern and eastern surbubs.

Hence, homebuyers in Melbourne, should be purchasing homes in around northern and western suburbs.
