Melbourne Housing Prices (2015-2020)

The dataset used in this project is sourced from Kaggle, comprising Melbourne real estate data from Domain.com.au. It offers detailed information about property address, type, price, location, and more. This dataset assists in finding affordable housing in metropolitan Melbourne by enabling users to analyze property prices and key metrics, aiding informed decisions for investors and homebuyers.

Choropleth Map of Metropolitan Melbourne

The map highlights Melbourne's average property price range, with Boroondara, Bayside, and Stonnington council areas being the most expensive, while Moorabool and Melton City Council areas are the least expensive, as they are located in Greater Melbourne.

Affordability in Metro Melbourne

Treemap of Median House Prices

Further insights gleaned from the treemap highlight the top suburbs in metropolitan Melbourne with the highest average home prices from each region in Melbourne (East,South,West,North and South East), shedding light on the areas where affordability may be particularly challenging.

Trends across different Home Attributes

In Melbourne, the pursuit of affordable housing is a multifaceted challenge, as reflected in key trends observed in the available data. The scatterplot analysis reveals a noteworthy correlation between the number of bedrooms and home prices, with an increase in the number of bedrooms often resulting in higher property costs.

Additionally, the scatterplot indicating that home prices tend to rise as properties draw closer to the city center underscores the impact of location on affordability.

Analysing Trends of Different Regions

The radial plot analysis highlights Boroondara as having the highest average house prices in Victoria, potentially due to its robust infrastructure, resources, schools, and other amenities. Stonnington also exhibits high prices, likely for similar reasons. These findings emphasize the role of location-related factors in shaping property values in these areas.

The boxplot reflects similar data to the radial plot. However showing that Southern suburbs are selling homes more expensive on a monthly scale.

This analysis shows that homes are more affordable in Western and Northern suburbs.

Histogram of Melbourne Median Hosue Prices by Region

Finally we can see that in the Eastern subrubs the price of selling at most around $800,000, Western suburbs homes are selling for at most $650,000, Northern suburbs are selling for around $750,000, Southern suburbs are selling for around $600,000-$800,000 and most properties in South Eastern suburbs are selling for around $600,000-$650,000.
